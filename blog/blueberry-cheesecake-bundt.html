Blueberry Cheesecake Bundt

My cinnamon roll dough, reworked into a bundt with a little less oil and spiraled with a vegan cheesecake swirl and blueberry filling.

Skip to recipe ↓

This started as a shortcut and turned into one of my favourite bakes. It’s the exact same enriched, psyllium-bound dough from my cinnamon rolls — the one that’s soft enough to roll into a two-foot sheet but structured enough to hold a tight spiral. Instead of cinnamon sugar, you spiral in two fillings: a quick vegan cheesecake swirl and a jammy blueberry filling. Then it goes into a bundt pan and bakes into a pull-apart ring that slices like a cake.

One change to the dough: a little less oil. The cinnamon rolls carry all their richness in the dough. Here the cheesecake swirl brings its own fat and moisture, so if you keep the oil where it was, the dough goes slack and the spirals lose their edges. Pulling it back from 115g to 95g keeps the crumb tender without turning greasy under the filling.

What Each Part Is Doing

Three components, each with a job. Get the fillings cold and the rest is just the cinnamon roll method in a different pan.

The Dough (with Less Oil)

Tapioca and potato starch give stretch, oat and rice flour give body, and psyllium is the structure — it’s what lets a gluten-free dough roll thin and hold a spiral through a long proof. The oil reduction is the only change from the cinnamon roll formula. Less oil means the dough stays firm enough to keep clean, distinct swirls once the wet cheesecake layer goes on.

The Cheesecake Swirl

This is deliberately simple: vegan cream cheese, a little sugar, and coconut milk to loosen it into something spreadable. The one addition is a spoon of cornstarch. Cream cheese and coconut milk are both mostly water, and without a binder that water weeps into the dough and steams the spiral soft. The cornstarch sets as it bakes and holds the swirl as a distinct layer instead of a wet streak.

The Blueberry Filling

This is my no-cook blueberry pie filling, and it does double duty here. Thawed frozen blueberries get blitzed with maple, cane sugar, lemon, vanilla, and salt, thickened by two things working together: psyllium husk grabs the berry juice and gives the filling body while it’s still raw and cold, and cornstarch sets it firm during the bake so the swirl holds instead of bleeding out. The lemon and salt aren’t for flavour of their own — they keep the berries from tasting flat and one-note sweet. Let it sit a few minutes after mixing so the psyllium can grab before it goes on the dough.

Watch Out — Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Keep the cheesecake swirl cold, and let the blueberry filling sit and thicken after mixing. A warm or runny filling melts the dough and steams it soft.
  • Leave a border along one long edge so the fillings don’t squeeze out when you roll.
  • Do not roll too tight — the spirals need room to expand during proofing.
  • Grease the bundt pan aggressively, into every ridge, or the sugar in the filling will weld it in place.
  • Never eat gluten-free baked goods warm. Always cool completely first, or it tastes gummy even when fully baked.

Choose Your Method

The dough amounts below are the hybrid method, which uses both sourdough starter and yeast. To make the faster single-leavening version, keep everything else the same and apply this adjustment:

Full yeast version (proof time: ~1.5 hours): remove the sourdough starter (192g), add 96g water, add 96g rice flour, and increase yeast to 3 tsp.

Instructions

  1. Gel the psyllium. Put the 300g lukewarm water in a bowl first, then sprinkle the psyllium husk over it while stirring immediately — it gels fast and clumps if you’re slow. Immersion-blend until smooth and set aside.
  2. Mix the wet ingredients. In a bowl, combine coconut sugar, maple syrup, sunflower oil, coconut milk, vanilla, sourdough starter, and yeast. Mix well.
  3. Mix the dry ingredients. In a separate bowl, whisk tapioca flour, potato starch, rice flour, oat flour, xanthan gum, baking powder, and sea salt until completely uniform.
  4. Bring the dough together. Add the gelled psyllium, all wet ingredients, and all dry ingredients to a stand mixer with the hook. Mix until fully incorporated. It will look slightly sticky — that is normal. Do not add flour.
  5. Hydrate. Rest the dough 10 minutes, then refrigerate at least 1 hour. Overnight is better — the longer it hydrates, the less it sticks when you roll.
  6. Make the cheesecake swirl. Beat the vegan cream cheese with cane sugar until smooth. Beat in the coconut milk, cornstarch, and vanilla until thick and spreadable. Keep it cold until you need it.
  7. Make the blueberry filling. Let the frozen blueberries defrost for about 20 minutes. Add everything — blueberries, maple syrup, cane sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, psyllium husk, vanilla, and salt — to a small mixer. Mix on low for 3 minutes, then scrape the sides and beater and mix on medium for 2 minutes. Let it sit a few minutes so the psyllium sets it to a spreadable, mounding consistency.
  8. Roll out the dough. Generously flour your counter. Roll the dough into a rectangle roughly 24 inches long by 12 inches wide, lifting it and adding flour underneath as you go.
  9. Layer the fillings. Spread the cold cheesecake swirl edge to edge, working from the centre outward so you don’t reshape the rectangle. Dollop the thickened blueberry filling over it in a thin, even layer and spread gently, leaving a 1-inch border along one long edge so it doesn’t squeeze out.
  10. Shape. Slice the sheet into 2-inch strips first, then roll each into a spiral with some give — do not roll tight. Grease a bundt pan well, into every ridge. Nestle the spirals cut-side up around the ring, pressing each against its neighbour so they hold together as a pull-apart bundt.
  11. Proof. Cover loosely with a cloth or plastic wrap — not airtight. Let rise at room temperature until puffy: about 3 hours for the hybrid method, about 1.5 hours for the full-yeast version.
  12. Bake. Preheat the oven to 350°F for at least 20 minutes. Bake the bundt for 40–45 minutes, until golden and set through. If the top browns before the centre is done, tent it loosely with foil.
  13. Cool. Non-negotiable with gluten-free baking. Cool in the pan for 20 minutes, then invert onto a rack and cool completely, at least another 35 minutes. Warm, it will taste gummy even when fully baked.
  14. Glaze. Whisk the powdered sugar, softened vegan cream cheese, coconut milk, and vanilla until smooth and pourable. Drizzle over the fully cooled bundt.

Because of the cheesecake swirl, this one lives in the fridge — keeps 3–4 days covered. Bring slices to room temperature, or warm them briefly, before serving.

Common Failures and Fixes

  • Filling squeezed out and the swirl disappeared? The fillings were too warm, or you rolled too tight. Chill both fillings hard, leave the border, and roll with some give.
  • Soggy, undercooked-looking streaks inside? Either the cheesecake swirl weeped (don’t skip the cornstarch), or you cut into it warm. Cool it completely before slicing.
  • Bundt stuck in the pan? The sugar in the filling caramelises onto the metal. Grease every ridge generously, and invert while still warm from the pan — after the 20-minute rest, not cold.
  • Spirals collapsed or fused flat? Over-proofed. Proof only until puffy, not doubled — gluten-free dough doesn’t recover from going past its peak.
  • Blueberry filling runny in the slice? Give it more time to set after mixing — the psyllium needs a few minutes to grab — and don’t skip the cornstarch, which firms it up in the bake. Spread it thin, not thick.
